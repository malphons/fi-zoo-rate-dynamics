Key Innovation

θ(t) — Time-Dependent Drift

Unlike Vasicek's constant drift, Hull-White uses θ(t) which is calibrated so the model exactly reproduces today's observed yield curve. This means: no arbitrage at time zero.

Calibration Formula

θ(t) = F_t(0,t) + aF(0,t) + (σ²/2a)(1-e^(-2at))
where F(0,t) is the instantaneous forward rate from the market curve.

Trinomial Tree

Hull-White is often implemented via a trinomial tree for pricing American/Bermudan options. The tree naturally accommodates the time-varying θ(t).

Why Not Just Vasicek?

Vasicek uses constant parameters and typically cannot match the observed yield curve. The gap creates arbitrage.

No-Arbitrage Pricing

Because Hull-White fits the term structure exactly, derivative prices are consistent with market bond prices. Vasicek would introduce basis risk due to the yield curve mismatch.

Common Applications

Swaption pricing, Bermudan callable bonds, CVA/DVA calculations, mortgage-backed securities. Any product where the current term structure matters for pricing.
